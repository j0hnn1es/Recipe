Ugali Managu Beef Recipe

This dish is a local favourite cuisine in Kenya, it's also easy to prepare.

Recipe information:

Preparation time is roughly 20-30 minutes.

Cooking time is 2 hours.removing lid for the last 10-15 minutes can help ticken the beef sauce

Number of servings is 4 people.

Difficulty level : Intermediate.

Ingredients:

For Beef:

  • 500g beef cubes.
  • 1 onion, chopped.
  • 1 tbsp garlic, minced.
  • 1 tbsp curry powder.
  • Salt and Pepper.
  • 3 tomatoes, diced.
  • 4 cups of water.
  • Coriander leaves, chopped.

For Ugali:

  • 4 cups water.
  • 2 cups maize flour (Unga wa Dola).

For Managu(African Nightshade):

  • 500g Managu (African Nightshade leaves),chopped.
  • 1 bunch of spinach leaves, chopped.
  • 2 tomatoes, diced.
  • 1 onion, diced.
  • 6 tbsp cooking cream (optional).
  • 1 tbsp black Pepper, powdered.
  • 1 tbsp chilli flakes.
  • 1 tbsp turmeric powder.
  • 1 tbsp ground cumin.
  • 1 tbsp ground coriander.
  • 1 tbsp seasoning (Royco).
  • Salt to taste.

Instructions:

For Beef:

  1. Heat oil in a heavy-based pot. Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  2. Add garlic and sauté for another minute.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes.
  4. Stir in the curry powder, salt, and pepper.
  5. Add the beef cubes and water. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer until the beef is tender.
  6. Garnish with chopped coriander leaves before serving.

For Ugali:

  1. In a separate pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil.
  2. Gradually add 2 cups of maize flour, stirring continuously to avoid lumps.
  3. Cook until the mixture thickens and pulls away from the sides of the pot.
  4. Reduce heat and cover, allowing it to steam for a few minutes.

For Managu:

  1. Heat oil in a pan or sufuria.
  2. Add the diced onions and sauté until tender and slightly brown.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes and cook until broken down into a paste.
  4. Add the chopped African Nightshade (Managu) and spinach leaves.
  5. Stir in the cooking cream (if using),add the spices, seasoning, and salt.
  6. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the greens are wilted and tender.
